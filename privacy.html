Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 18, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how onFIRE ("the Service," "we," "our," or "us") collects, uses, stores, and protects information when you use our personal financial independence tracking application. onFIRE is operated by an individual developer based in Idaho, USA.

By using onFIRE, you agree to the practices described in this policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the Service.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Account Information

When you create an account, we may collect your email address and any optional profile information you choose to provide.

1.2 Financial Account Data (via Plaid)

onFIRE uses Plaid Inc. to connect to your financial institutions. When you link an account, you authenticate directly with your bank or brokerage through Plaid's secure interface. onFIRE never sees, receives, or stores your banking credentials (username, password, security answers, or MFA codes).

Depending on the accounts you link and the Plaid products enabled, onFIRE may receive:

  • Account metadata (institution name, account type, account nickname, masked account number)
  • Account balances (current and available)
  • Transaction history (date, amount, merchant, category)
  • Investment holdings and investment transactions
  • Liability details (loan balances, interest rates, payment schedules)

Plaid's role: Plaid acts as a data processor on your behalf. Plaid's own handling of your data is governed by its End User Privacy Policy. You can review and manage the financial connections you've authorized through Plaid at my.plaid.com.

1.3 Usage Data

We may collect basic technical information when you use the Service, including device type, browser, IP address, and interaction logs. This data is used to operate and improve the Service.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect solely to:

  • Provide the core Service — aggregating account data, calculating net worth, and tracking progress toward financial independence goals you set
  • Authenticate you and maintain your account
  • Communicate with you about the Service, including security notices
  • Maintain, debug, and improve the Service
  • Comply with legal obligations

3. What We Do Not Do

  • We do not sell your personal or financial data. Ever.
  • We do not share your data with advertisers or use it for advertising.
  • We do not share your data with third parties for their marketing purposes.
  • We do not access your data for any purpose other than operating the Service for you.

4. Data Sharing

We only share your information in the limited circumstances below:

  • Service providers: We use trusted third-party infrastructure providers (such as Plaid for financial data access and cloud hosting providers for data storage) who process data on our behalf under confidentiality obligations.
  • Legal compliance: We may disclose information if required by law, subpoena, or court order, or if necessary to protect the rights, safety, or property of users or the public.
  • With your consent: We will share information in any other way only with your explicit, informed consent.

5. Data Storage and Security

Your data is stored on cloud infrastructure with industry-standard security controls, including encryption in transit (TLS) and encryption at rest. Access is restricted to authorized operations on your behalf.

While we take reasonable measures to protect your information, no method of electronic storage or transmission is completely secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security, and you use the Service at your own risk.

6. Data Retention

We retain your data for as long as your account is active. You may disconnect linked financial accounts or delete your account at any time, at which point we will delete your associated data within 30 days, except where retention is required by law.

7. Your Rights and Choices

You have the following rights regarding your information:

  • Access: Request a copy of the data we hold about you
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your account and associated data
  • Disconnection: Unlink any financial institution connection at any time within the Service or directly through my.plaid.com
  • Portability: Request an export of your data in a machine-readable format

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at truexclaude@gmail.com.

8. California and Other U.S. State Privacy Rights

If you are a resident of California, Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, Utah, or another U.S. state with comprehensive privacy legislation, you have additional rights under applicable state law, including the right to know, delete, correct, and opt out of certain processing. We do not sell personal information as defined by these laws. To exercise state-specific rights, contact us at the email above.

9. Children's Privacy

onFIRE is not intended for use by individuals under 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect information from children under 18. If you believe a child has provided us information, please contact us and we will delete it.

10. International Users

onFIRE is operated from the United States. If you access the Service from outside the U.S., your information will be transferred to, stored, and processed in the United States, which may have different data protection laws than your country of residence.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we will notify you by updating the "Last updated" date above and, where appropriate, through in-app notice or email. Continued use of the Service after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
